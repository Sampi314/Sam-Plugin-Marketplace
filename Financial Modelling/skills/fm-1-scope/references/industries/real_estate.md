# Real Estate & Property

## Typical Revenue Drivers
- **Gross rental income** — per sqm or per unit, by lease type
- **Vacancy rate** — typically 3-10% for commercial; varies by market
- **Lease-up period** — new developments take time to reach stabilised occupancy
- **Tenant incentives** — rent-free periods, fitout contributions (amortised over lease term)
- **Rent reviews** — CPI-linked, fixed %, market review; timing varies by lease
- **Other income** — parking, signage, recoverable outgoings

## Cost Drivers to Suggest
- Property management fees (% of gross income)
- Non-recoverable outgoings (council rates, insurance, land tax)
- Recoverable outgoings (passed through to tenants)
- Leasing costs (agent commission on new leases)
- Maintenance and repairs
- Capital improvements

## CapEx Considerations
- Development / construction cost
- Land acquisition (stamp duty, legals)
- DA and approval costs
- Fitout allowances
- Settlement timing (staged settlements for off-plan)

## Key Metrics
- Cap rate (Net Operating Income / Value)
- IRR (development and investment)
- Development margin (profit / total cost)
- Yield on cost
- WALE (Weighted Average Lease Expiry)
- Debt yield, LVR

## Common Pitfalls
- Not staggering lease expiries (cliff-edge risk)
- Forgetting tenant incentive amortisation
- Static vacancy instead of modelling lease-by-lease
- Not distinguishing between gross and net rent
- Land tax calculations (progressive rates, aggregation)
