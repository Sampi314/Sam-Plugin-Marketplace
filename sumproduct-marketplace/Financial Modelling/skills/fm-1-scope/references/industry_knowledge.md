## Mining & Resources

### Typical Revenue Drivers
- **Production volume** — linked to mine plan (ore body, strip ratio, processing capacity)
- **Commodity price** — spot vs contracted vs hedged; consider multiple commodities if polymetallic
- **Grade and recovery** — ore grade declines over mine life; processing recovery rate
- **Ramp-up** — production ramp from commissioning to nameplate capacity (typically 6–24 months)

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- Mining costs ($/tonne moved — overburden + ore)
- Processing costs ($/tonne processed)
- Haulage and transport
- Royalties (state/federal — often % of revenue or % of profit, varies by jurisdiction)
- Environmental levies
- Rehabilitation/closure provisioning (accrue over mine life, release at closure)

### CapEx Considerations
- Pre-production / development CapEx (often 2–5 years before revenue)
- Sustaining CapEx (ongoing maintenance, equipment replacement)
- Mine infrastructure (haul roads, water, power, tailings dams)
- Processing plant (staged or single build?)

### Key Metrics
- NPV, IRR (pre-tax and post-tax)
- Payback period
- All-in sustaining cost (AISC) per unit
- Life-of-mine cash flow
- Ore reserve to production ratio (mine life)

### Common Pitfalls
- Forgetting rehabilitation provisioning entirely
- Using mine life in years instead of linking to depleting ore reserves
- Not modelling the strip ratio (overburden increases as mine deepens)
- Static commodity price when scenarios should test price volatility
- Tax — mining-specific royalties and resource rent taxes

## Energy & Renewables

### Typical Revenue Drivers
- **Installed capacity** (MW)
- **Capacity factor** — varies by technology: solar ~20-30%, onshore wind ~25-40%, offshore wind ~35-50%, gas ~80-95%
- **Generation** (MWh) = Capacity × Capacity factor × Hours in period
- **Degradation** — solar panels degrade ~0.5%/year; wind turbines less but maintenance increases
- **Revenue structure** — PPA (contracted price) vs merchant (spot market) vs hybrid
- **RECs / carbon credits** — additional revenue stream, subject to policy risk

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- O&M (fixed $/MW + variable $/MWh)
- Land lease / rental
- Grid connection charges and transmission losses
- Insurance
- Inverter/battery replacement (solar — typically year 10-15)
- Curtailment risk (grid constraints may force output reduction)

### CapEx Considerations
- EPC contract (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) — often lump sum
- Grid connection costs (can be 10-20% of total CapEx)
- Development costs (pre-FID)
- Battery storage (if co-located)

### Key Metrics
- Levellised Cost of Energy (LCOE) — $/MWh
- NPV, IRR (equity and project)
- DSCR (for debt-financed projects)
- Payback period
- Capacity factor sensitivity

### Common Pitfalls
- Assuming constant capacity factor (it varies by season and degrades over time)
- Forgetting curtailment (grid can't always absorb output)
- PPA escalation not matching inflation assumption
- Not modelling merchant tail after PPA expires
- Battery degradation cycles vs calendar degradation

## Infrastructure & PPP

### Typical Revenue Drivers
- **Availability payments** — government pays for asset being available, not usage
- **Patronage / usage** — tolls, fares, throughput volume
- **Abatement / penalty regime** — deductions for unavailability or KPI failures
- **Concession term** — 20-35 years typical; asset reverts to government at expiry

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- Lifecycle / major maintenance (lumpy, not annual — model as reserves)
- Operating costs (facilities management, maintenance, utilities)
- Handback provisions (asset must meet condition standards at concession end)
- Insurance (construction all-risks, operational)
- SPV overhead (audit, legal, trustee, agent fees)

### CapEx Considerations
- Construction cost (D&C contract — often design & construct lump sum)
- Construction period (2-5 years)
- Fitout / equipment
- Land acquisition / compulsory purchase
- Client-side costs during construction

### Key Metrics
- Equity IRR (pre-tax and post-tax)
- DSCR, LLCR, PLCR (lender covenants)
- Annual Debt Service Cover Ratio profile
- Cash available for distribution to equity
- NPV of equity cash flows

### Common Pitfalls
- Circular reference from interest on cash balance (need average balance approach)
- Not modelling the cash waterfall correctly (debt service priority over equity)
- Lifecycle reserve not sized correctly (lenders require 6-12 months forward cover)
- Handback provisions not escalated for inflation
- Abatement regime complexity (multiple KPIs with different weightings)

## Real Estate & Property

### Typical Revenue Drivers
- **Gross rental income** — per sqm or per unit, by lease type
- **Vacancy rate** — typically 3-10% for commercial; varies by market
- **Lease-up period** — new developments take time to reach stabilised occupancy
- **Tenant incentives** — rent-free periods, fitout contributions (amortised over lease term)
- **Rent reviews** — CPI-linked, fixed %, market review; timing varies by lease
- **Other income** — parking, signage, recoverable outgoings

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- Property management fees (% of gross income)
- Non-recoverable outgoings (council rates, insurance, land tax)
- Recoverable outgoings (passed through to tenants)
- Leasing costs (agent commission on new leases)
- Maintenance and repairs
- Capital improvements

### CapEx Considerations
- Development / construction cost
- Land acquisition (stamp duty, legals)
- DA and approval costs
- Fitout allowances
- Settlement timing (staged settlements for off-plan)

### Key Metrics
- Cap rate (Net Operating Income / Value)
- IRR (development and investment)
- Development margin (profit / total cost)
- Yield on cost
- WALE (Weighted Average Lease Expiry)
- Debt yield, LVR

### Common Pitfalls
- Not staggering lease expiries (cliff-edge risk)
- Forgetting tenant incentive amortisation
- Static vacancy instead of modelling lease-by-lease
- Not distinguishing between gross and net rent
- Land tax calculations (progressive rates, aggregation)

## Technology & SaaS

### Typical Revenue Drivers
- **MRR / ARR** (Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue)
- **New customer acquisition** — per month/quarter
- **Churn rate** — % customers lost per period (gross vs net churn)
- **ARPU** (Average Revenue Per User) — by plan tier
- **Expansion revenue** — upsell/cross-sell within existing base
- **Professional services / implementation** — one-time

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- Cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure/GCP — often % of revenue, scales with usage)
- Engineering headcount (largest cost — salary + benefits + equity comp)
- Sales & marketing (CAC — Customer Acquisition Cost)
- G&A (rent, legal, insurance)
- R&D capitalisation vs expensing

### Key Metrics
- LTV/CAC ratio (>3x is healthy)
- CAC payback period
- Net revenue retention (NRR)
- Gross margin (should be >70% for SaaS)
- Burn rate and runway (months of cash remaining)
- Rule of 40 (growth rate + profit margin ≥ 40%)

### Common Pitfalls
- Not modelling cohort dynamics (each cohort has its own churn curve)
- Confusing gross churn with net churn
- Forgetting that cloud costs scale with usage, not revenue
- Stock-based compensation as a real cost, not just a footnote
- Working capital timing (annual contracts paid upfront → deferred revenue)

## Retail & Consumer

### Typical Revenue Drivers
- **Store count** — existing + new openings schedule
- **Revenue per store** — maturity curve (new stores ramp up)
- **Same-store sales growth** (SSSG)
- **Average transaction value** × transactions per day
- **Online vs physical split** — different margins and cost structures
- **Seasonality** — critical for monthly models (Christmas, Easter, etc.)

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- COGS (% of revenue — by product category)
- Store labour (shifts × hourly rate)
- Rent and occupancy (fixed + % of turnover for some leases)
- Inventory management (shrinkage, markdowns, obsolescence)
- Supply chain / logistics
- Marketing (% of revenue or fixed campaign budget)

### Key Metrics
- EBITDA margin (by store and consolidated)
- Same-store sales growth
- Store-level ROI
- Inventory turns
- Payback period on new stores

## Government & NFP

### Typical Revenue/Funding Drivers
- Grant funding (tranche-based, milestone-linked)
- Government appropriation (annual budget allocation)
- User fees / charges
- Cost recovery mechanisms
- Donations / fundraising

### Cost Drivers to Suggest
- Staff costs (EBA/award rates, superannuation, leave provisions)
- Program delivery costs
- Compliance and reporting costs
- Overhead allocation

### Key Considerations
- **Funding conditions** — acquittal requirements, eligible expenditure
- **Cash vs accrual** — government often reports on cash basis
- **Forward estimates** — 3-4 year horizon typical
- **Efficiency dividends** — mandated cost reductions

## Project Finance

> Cross-sector patterns common to any project-financed deal.

### Debt Structure to Suggest
- **Senior debt** — term loan with sculpted repayments to DSCR targets
- **Cash sweep** — excess cash applied to accelerated repayment
- **Reserve accounts** — Debt Service Reserve (6 months), Maintenance Reserve, Distribution Lock-up
- **Cash waterfall** — strict priority of payments (opex → debt service → reserves → equity)
- **Lock-up test** — distributions to equity only if DSCR > threshold (typically 1.10-1.20x)
- **Default test** — if DSCR < 1.00x, technical default

### Key Ratios
- **DSCR** — CFADS ÷ Debt Service (target 1.20-1.50x depending on sector)
- **LLCR** — NPV of remaining CFADS ÷ Outstanding Debt
- **PLCR** — NPV of project life CFADS ÷ Outstanding Debt
- **Gearing** — Debt / (Debt + Equity) at financial close

### Common Pitfalls
- Circularity from interest on cash balance
- Not sculpting repayments (flat repayments waste debt capacity)
- Cash waterfall order wrong (equity before reserves)
- Forgetting that DSCR is a period-by-period test, not an average
- Tax timing differences (cash tax vs accounting tax)

## General Corporate

### Standard 3-Statement Structure
- Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → OpEx → EBITDA → D&A → EBIT → Interest → PBT → Tax → NPAT
- BS: CA + NCA = CL + NCL + Equity
- CFS: Operating + Investing + Financing = Net Cash Movement

### Working Capital Drivers
- Days Sales Outstanding (receivable days)
- Days Payable Outstanding (payable days)
- Days Inventory Outstanding (inventory days)
- Cash conversion cycle = DSO + DIO - DPO

### Key Metrics
- Revenue growth, gross margin, EBITDA margin, NPAT margin
- ROE, ROA, ROIC
- EPS, P/E
- Free Cash Flow, FCF yield
- Net Debt / EBITDA
